Rush

ABOUT

Rush is a Canadian rock band that was originally formed in Toronto in 1968. The original band members were Alex Lifeson, John Rutsey, and Jeff Jones. Jones was quickly replaced by Geddy Lee who specialized in bass, vocals, keyboards, and music composition. Then, in 1974, Rutsey was replaced by drummer Neil Peart who went on to become legendary as Rush continued their commercial success throughout the 1970's, 80's, and 90's.

The band was active from 1968-2018, and during these years they were awarded 14 platinum and 3 multi-platinum albums in the United States, as well as 17 platinum albums in Canada. Rush was also nominated for 7 Grammy Awards, won many Juno awards, and also won the International Achievement Award at the 2009 SOCAN Awards. Rush was Inducted into the Canadian Music Hall of Fame in 1994, and were later inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2013.
